Personality Types

The ancient theory of the four temperaments—Choleric, Sanguine, Melancholy, and Phlegmatic—categorizes people based on their natural tendencies and emotional dispositions. While this model is largely theoretical and often simplified, it offers an interesting lens to view personalities. Explore each temperament to discover key traits and real-life examples from politics, sports, and business.

Choleric Personality

Known for being ambitious, assertive, and decisive, Cholerics are natural leaders who thrive in fast-paced environments.

Sanguine Personality

Sanguines are sociable, outgoing, and enthusiastic, often bringing joy and energy to their environments.

Melancholy Personality

Analytical and thoughtful, Melancholics are detail-oriented, often focusing on perfection and quality in all they do.

Phlegmatic Personality

Calm, patient, and harmonious, Phlegmatics are great peacemakers and often bring stability and balance to any situation.
